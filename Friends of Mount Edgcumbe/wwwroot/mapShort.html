Map of the Mount Edgcumbe Country Park Short Walk

Guide

Leaving Lambert Lodge, walk through the formal gardens to view items 1 – 5. Enter the Italian Garden (1) where the Friends have sponsored the bedding and the orange trees and funded the repairs to the statues on the Belvedere. Pass in front of the Orangery and on reaching the grass, cut across to enter the Golden Jubilee Garden (2) with its sound sculpture. Detour to the left to view the Black Bee apiary (3) and then retrace your steps to continue through the Silver Jubilee Garden. Turn left around the French conservatory into the French Garden (4) where the Friends have sponsored the bedding and repaired both the fountain and the conservatory. At the far end of the French Garden turn right and right again into the Relic Garden (5) which was totally funded by the Friends. After leaving the Relic Garden, turn left and left again to leave the formal gardens as indicated on the map. After visiting the Relic Garden continue through the hedge towards the sea and turn right, leaving the formal gardens. Continue across Barn Pool and pick up the coastal path towards the duck pond. At the duck pond, stay on the concrete track as it winds its way up through the amphitheatre, noticing The New Trees (6) that were planted in 2020. At the T-junction, for the short walk (1.25km), turn right and follow the path back up to the house where you can visit items 10 – 16. The Barrow Centre (10) was developed with help from the Friends and now houses retail outlets and The Farriers for food and drink. Take a look at the Batcam (11) which was funded by the Friends, as well as the Fire Auxiliary (12) and assorted memorabilia from old fire stations. When you have finished wandering around the Barrow Centre, leave in the direction of the house, passing the Deer Garden (13) on your way. Passing through the arch, turn right towards the house, through the Knot Garden (14). If the house is open, visit the Earl’s Garden and look at the Cedar Seat (15) and Shell Seat (16). Note: entrance fee payable except for members of the Friends. When you have finished, work your way back down the hill to Lambert Lodge (17), passing the children’s play area by Cremyll Gate (18) which was built with the Friends’ help.
